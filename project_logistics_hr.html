HR & Driver Performance Dashboard

Part of the Logistics Operations Analytics Suite. This report addresses the core operational question facing any logistics company: are we moving goods efficiently, and are our trucks performing as expected? It covers delivery performance, fleet utilization, maintenance cost analysis, and customer revenue. It is built on top of the Operations data mart in the LogisticsWarehouse SQL Server database.

The report covers January through October 2024 as the reporting period, reflecting a realistic year-to-date executive view.

Key Findings

  • Higher pricing drives top route performance

    Phoenix, AZ → Philadelphia, PA is the highest revenue route at $2.87M YTD, despite not ranking in the top routes by trip volume. This suggests higher pricing per load on the route rather than high frequency. This appears to be a margin-efficient route worth protecting and expanding.

  • 2018 model year trucks carry the highest maintenance cost per mile

    Despite not being the oldest trucks in the fleet, the 2018 trucks show the highest average maintenance cost per mile. This non-linear relationship between age and maintenance cost suggests a specific model or mechanical issue with that year's cohort rather than simple age-related wear.

  • Three trucks are consistently underperforming

    TRK00073, TRK00088, and TRK00044 appear in both the top maintenance cost ranking and the top downtime ranking. This pattern indicates chronic underperformance; these trucks are generating disproportionate costs while simultaneously reducing operational capacity through extended downtime.

  • Customer revenue is heavily concentrated

    First Group is the top customer at $2.62M, which is 41% above the second-highest customer. This pattern is also visible in previous years. While this reflects a strong account relationship, significant revenue concentration in a single customer introduces risk if that account reduces volume or transitions to a competitor.

  • Automotive and Food/Beverage dominate freight revenue

    Automotive freight generates $15.1M and Food/Beverage $13.7M, together accounting for 39% of total revenue. These two freight types represent the company's core business and anchor its operational capacity planning.

Recommendations

  • Prioritise the Phoenix → Philadelphia and Charlotte → Portland routes for capacity investment.

    Both combine high revenue with strong margins per load. Adding frequency or dedicated capacity on these routes would yield the highest return on investment.

  • Evaluate TRK00073, TRK00088, and TRK00044 for early retirement or major overhaul.

    The combination of high maintenance costs and high downtime makes these trucks net-negative contributors to fleet efficiency. A cost-benefit analysis comparing overhaul costs against replacement costs should be conducted before the next maintenance cycle.

  • Investigate 2018 model year trucks as a cohort.

    The elevated maintenance cost per mile is unlikely to be coincidental. Cross-referencing makes and models within that year group may reveal a specific mechanical pattern and inform future procurement decisions to avoid repeating the issue.

  • Reduce customer revenue concentration risk.

    With First Group accounting for a large share of revenue, the company should actively develop the next tier of customers, particularly those in the $1.1M–$1.5M range, to build a more resilient revenue base.

  • Strengthen capacity in Automotive and Food/Beverage freight.

    As the two dominant freight types, any capacity constraints in these segments would impact total revenue. Ensuring sufficient refrigerated and flatbed trailer availability for these freight types should be a fleet planning priority.
