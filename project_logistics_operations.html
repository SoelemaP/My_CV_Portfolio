Key Findings

1

Premium pricing drives top route performance

Phoenix AZ → Philadelphia PA is the highest revenue route at $2.87M YTD, despite not ranking in the top routes by trip volume. This suggests premium per-load pricing on this lane rather than high frequency — a margin-efficient route worth protecting and expanding.

2

2018 model year trucks carry the highest maintenance cost per mile

Despite not being the oldest trucks in the fleet, 2018 trucks show the highest average maintenance cost per mile. This non-linear relationship between age and maintenance cost suggests a specific model or mechanical issue with that year's cohort rather than simple age-related wear.

3

Three trucks are consistently underperforming

TRK00073, TRK00088, and TRK00044 appear in both the top maintenance cost ranking and the top downtime ranking. This pattern indicates chronic underperformance — these trucks are generating disproportionate cost while simultaneously reducing operational capacity through extended downtime.

4

Customer revenue is heavily concentrated

First Group is the top customer at $2.62M — 41% above the second highest customer. While this reflects a strong account relationship, significant revenue concentration in a single customer introduces risk if that account reduces volume or transitions to a competitor.

5

Automotive and Food/Beverage dominate freight revenue

Automotive freight generates $15.1M and Food/Beverage $13.7M — together accounting for 39% of total revenue. These two freight types represent the company's core business and anchor its operational capacity planning.

Recommendations

Prioritise the Phoenix → Philadelphia and Charlotte → Portland lanes for capacity investment. Both combine high revenue with strong per-load margins. Adding frequency or dedicated capacity on these routes would yield the highest return on operational investment.
Evaluate TRK00073, TRK00088, and TRK00044 for early retirement or major overhaul. The combination of high maintenance cost and high downtime makes these trucks net-negative contributors to fleet efficiency. A cost-benefit analysis comparing overhaul cost against replacement cost should be conducted before the next maintenance cycle.
Investigate 2018 model year trucks as a cohort. The elevated maintenance cost per mile is unlikely to be coincidental. Cross-referencing make and model within that year group may reveal a specific mechanical pattern — and inform future procurement decisions to avoid repeating the issue.
Reduce customer revenue concentration risk. With First Group accounting for a disproportionate share of revenue, the company should actively develop the next tier of customers — particularly those in the $1.1M–$1.5M range — to build a more resilient revenue base.
Strengthen capacity in Automotive and Food/Beverage freight. As the two dominant freight types, any capacity constraints in these segments would disproportionately impact total revenue. Ensuring sufficient refrigerated and flatbed trailer availability for these freight types should be a fleet planning priority.
