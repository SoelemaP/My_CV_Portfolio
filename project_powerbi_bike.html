Customer Purchase Behavior & Lead Generation Dashboard

Project Description

The Problem

The company faced low sales and an inefficient marketing strategy. Without a clear understanding of their Ideal Customer Profile, the sales team was unable to prioritize leads, leading to wasted resources. The core challenge was: Where should the company invest its next marketing and sales campaign for the highest ROI?

The Solution

I developed a multi-page interactive Power BI dashboard that transformed raw Excel data into a strategic roadmap. By cross-referencing demographic data with historical purchase success, I built a logic-based filtering system. This culminated in a Top 20 Lead List, identifying specific individuals who fit the "Buyer Sweet Spot" but have not yet made a purchase.

Analysis: Key Findings

  • Lifestyle Triggers: Homeownership and short commute distances (0-1 miles) are the strongest predictors of purchase intent.
  • Friction Points: High car ownership (3+ vehicles) and long commutes significantly decrease the likelihood of a bike purchase.
  • Target Demographics: While education and occupation have broad appeal, the "Middle Aged" and "Adult" segments with 0-2 cars provide the highest conversion stability.
  • Geographic Opportunity: The Pacific and North American regions house the highest concentration of high-income non-buyers, representing the best "growth" markets.

Technical Tools & Future Growth

Tools Used

  • Power BI: Used for data analysis and visualization.
  • Excel: Datasource: Bikers Database.

Future Growth

  • Time-Series Analysis: Integrating historical sales dates to identify seasonal buying trends (e.g., Summer vs. Winter).
  • Predictive Modeling: Using the identified triggers to build a simple machine learning model that "scores" new leads as High, Medium, or Low priority.
  • CRM Integration: Automating the "Top 20" list to refresh daily, allowing the sales team to see new high-value prospects in real-time.
